Spanish Geography

The Location of Spain

At 505,992 km² Spain is Europe's fourth largest country. Mount Teide at 3,718 m is the highest mountain peak in Spain and is the third largest volcano in the world from its base. Spain is a transcontinental country, having territory in both Europe and Africa.

On the west, Spain is bordered by Portugal; on the south, it is bordered by Gibraltar and Morocco, through its exclaves in North Africa. On the northeast, along the Pyrenees mountain range, it is bordered by France and Andorra. Extending to 1,214 km the Portugal–Spain border is the longest uninterrupted border within the European Union. The vegetation of Spain is varied due to several factors including the diversity of the relief, the climate and latitude. Spain has many different regions, each with its own floristic characteristics resulting largely from the interaction of climate, topography, soil type and fire and biotic factors.

Climate

Spain is dominated by Mediterranean climate. At the south coast of Spain, there is the Hot Summer Typical Mediterranean climate. This has hot dry summers and mild winters with temperatures averaging around 20°C to 30 °C over the year.

Inland, there is a bigger difference in temperatures. Summers are hotter and Winters are colder. There is no Mediterranean air to help regulate the temps. Here the summers can go up to 35°C and the winters as low as 0°C.

The north and west of Spain have an oceanic climate. The climate and landscape are determined by the Atlantic Ocean winds whose moisture gets trapped by the mountains circumventing the Spanish Atlantic coast. Temperatures are more mild at around 10°C to 20°C over the course of the year.
