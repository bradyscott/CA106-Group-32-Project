French Cuisine is notoriously famous for its exquisite, elegant dishes that complements all elements on a plate. French Cuisines consists of cooking traditions that have been developed, improved upon to deliver world famous food that is renowned for its taste! So as they in France Bon Apetit Monsieur/Madame!

A common element of French Cuisine is the tradition revolving outdoor dining. It’s no secret that the French love outdoor dining. Therefore, on most side streets/promenades, it won’t be uncommon to find for example the following:

  1. Bistros
  2. Cafes
  3. Restaurants

French cuisine is very popular across the globe. Many dishes that originated from France, has seen a widespread adoption throughout the world. Below, you will find some of these dishes!!

Crepes

The French Crepe is a very thin but packed full of flavour. To us a Crepe would mirror our famous pancakes. Crepes are a delicacy usually enjoyed after a flavoursum meal. Many times Crepes are stuffed with chocolate to add that extra little bit of flavour that many people love some much. This Crepes are usually garnished with fresh fruit and some icing sugar to decorate.

Ratatouille

Ratatouille has to be my favourite French delicacy of all time! Ratatouille is such a simple recipe to make, although when ready it looks like a meal served in a five star Restaurent. Ratatouille is essentially a stew. The main ingredients include tomato, garlic, onion, courgette with some other ingredients being added by other chefs. Some chefs include meat as an ingredient but its not mandatory.

Croissants

Croissants is one of the most common French delicacies that have seen a widespread adoption across the globe. These soft but sweet breakfast essential is a food item France will never be in short supply of. This cresenct shaped, flakey pastry, tipically enjoyed at breakfast time is a must have when visiting France! And what makes this delicacy even more enjoyable is croissants can aslo be stuffed with items such as chocolate and haslenut! Sometimes these breakfast delights can be stuffed with cheese and ham to a little more flavour!
