French Introduction

Source: Britannica.com

France has a long line of history. From the French Revolution to both World Wars and crisis within its own government, France have seen it all and have emerged stronger as a country.

The French Revolution was a movement that divided France between the years 1787 and 1799. Like most Revolutions there are the same simliar reasons. However, the French Revolution was the most violent of them all. For a decade, French Citizens destroyed their city in an attempt to overthrow power to regain indepemdence. The French Revolution came to an end when a general named Napoleon overthrew the government to establish independence.

The French Revolution had a profound impact on the hierachy structure that ruled France for so long. The French Revolution stripped powers form the Cathloic Church and put an end to the long reign of the French Monarchy. From The Revolution, ideas like liberty and freedom flourished throughout Europe (not just France) and paved the way to a better life for French Citizens.
