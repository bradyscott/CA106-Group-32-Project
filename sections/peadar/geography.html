Structure

New Zealands structure consists of two larger islands called the north and south islands and the rest is made up of roughly 600 islands. It's land area is about 270,000km squared. New Zealand is part of the continent Oceania and is located in the South Pacific Ocean.

Tectonic Plates

New Zealand's location is on the boundary of the Pacific and Indo-Australian tectonic plates, this has shaped its landforms. The results of being near these plates are a hilly and mountainous terrain, There is also frequent earthquakes in most parts of the country and there is a zone of volcanic and geothermal activity in the centre of the North Island.

Climate

New Zealands Climate is largely influenced by the surrounding ocean, mountains and tropical weather patterns. The average rainfall in urban areas is about 60cm - 1.5m a year but in more mountainous areas it often exceeds 5 meters. The South Island has the least amount of rainfall and can have less than 60cm of rain in the full year. Average temperatures in the far North are around 15°C and 10°C in the far south.
