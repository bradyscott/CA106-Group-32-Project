English is the predominant language in New Zealand with a total of 95% of the people using it as their first language. Most of the other 5% is the Māori language of the indigenous Māori people. New Zealand Sign language has also been an offical language of New Zealand since 2006.

The Māori Language

The Māori language is the first language the was spoken in New Zealand. At the beginning of the 19th century it was the predominant language in spoken in New Zealand. As more English speakers arrived the language started to die out. Major initiatives were launched to help stop the language from dieing and has brought a bit of a revival to the language. An estimate of roughly 125,000 people can now speak and understand the language.

Here are some common words in the Māori language:

  • New Zealand = Aotearoa
  • Hello = Tena koutou
  • Bye = Kia ora
  • Thanks = Whakawhetai
  • Sorry = Aroha mai
