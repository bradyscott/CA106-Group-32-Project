Icelandic is the official language of Iceland, being spoken by about 97% of the population. English is also widely spoken and understood, mainly becuase of the large number of English-speaking tourists that visit the country but also because of the fact that it is compulsory to learn both English and another Scandanavian langauge (often Danish) in Icelandic schools.

Basic Icelandic Phrases

Below, you can learn how to say a few basic phrases in Icelandic! Try listening to the audio.

  1. Hæ/Halló

    2. Pronounced: Hi/Hah-low

    Starting with the very basics, here are your generic greetings; both simply mean 'hello'. You use these the same way you would in the English language. Hæ is more common and it is often said twice in a greeting: Hæ hæ

    Hæ

    Halló

  2. Já

    3. Pronounced: y-ow

    This means 'yes'.

  3. Nei

    4. Pronounced: ney

    This means 'no'.

  4. Góðan daginn

    5. Pronounced: go-thah-n die-in

    The literal translation here is 'good day' and it is probably the most common greeting in the Icelandic language. Use this to say hello to all the people you meet and it will show them that you are both polite and friendly.

  5. Ég heiti

    6. Pronounced: ye-gh hey-tee

    This means 'My name is...'.

  6. Hvar er

    7. Pronounced: kva-r e-r

    This means 'Where is...?'.

  7. Klósett

    8. Pronounced: k-low-seht

    This simply means bathroom, or toilet.

  8. Pkve-r-su mih-kith coh-stah-r theh-tah

    9. Pronounced: kve-r-su mih-kith coh-stah-r theh-tah

    This means 'How much does this cost?' When shopping for food or souvenirs, it will be helpful to be able to ask how much an object costs. They may also use the word 'kall', which is the equivalent of 'bucks' in English.

  9. Þakka þér

    10. Pronounced: thah-kah th-yeh-r

    This is a formal way of saying 'thank you'.

  10. Verðu þér góður

    11. Pronounced: veh-r-thu th-yeh-r go-thu-r

    This basically means 'you are welcome'.

  11. Bless

    12. Pronounced: bleh-s

    This means 'goodbye' or simply 'bye'. This, like the greeting, is often repeated twice: 'Bless bless'.
